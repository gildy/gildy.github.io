Motivation

Our cultural behaviour in technology consumption is reducing our ability to communicate with one another in a meaningful way and making us more socially isolated. We are less genuinely connected with one another than in the past. Communication technologies around us are invented with a promise to lead us to a better life, by improving our relationships and helping us to keep in touch. However, we are becoming more socially isolated because the technology itself is too distracting.

Research suggests that technology is increasing loneliness and undermining empathy rather than helping, and can be harmful to our well-being. Surprisingly, social networks like Facebook that were originally created to connect people are promoting comparison and competition. As a result, instead of getting emotionally closer to each other, people are less happy with their lives, feel lonelier and are more envious of each other. However, the solution is not to avoid technology.

Giving more attention and awareness on emotion and empathy in the interaction design, grounded in solid research, could fix the current situation.

Imagine when we are facing a hard time at work. Knowing that there are people who are always there to support us means a lot to us, and can help us make it through the day and embrace the challenge of tomorrow.

Japanese people have their own unique gesture on empathy. In Japanese culture, Otsukaresama is a unique expression to show thoughts on empathy and appreciating someone’s effort. It represents thoughtfulness to someone, and a reward of their achievement, and it reflects the psychological nature of the human fear of loneliness and the desire for support from others. It is a powerful phrase that communicates appreciation, and cultivates the empathy.

What is the purpose of the study?

My aim is to develop a novel system to communicate empathy through technology, which will enable users to experience the same feeling of being appreciated, supported, and socially accepted after being greeted by a gesture of Otsukaresama

As a step of the design process, we would like to do an observation on daily ups and downs, to find out how do people currently give and receive forms and gestures of appreciation, support, and social acceptance. We want to study about what kind of technology that people are currently using as media to fulfil this need.

Why have I been invited?

We are interested to conduct the study on people from wide variety of background with regular routine of work or study across the week. There will be 8 participants, and each of the participants will be interacting with the diary system for one week.

Do I have to take part?

Participation in the project is voluntary. It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you do decide to take part, you will be asked to sign a consent form. If you decide to take part, you are still free to withdraw at any time and without giving a reason. You can withdraw at any stage of the project without being penalised or disadvantaged.

What do I have to do?

  • During your daily activities, you will be asked to interact with our virtual diary (in a form of mobile app) and you will press different buttons that correspond to your current feeling/situation (related to daily ups and downs). The time and location when the button is pressed will be recorded.
  • You will be encouraged to take at least 2 pictures per day of anything (object, scenery, event, or person) that have significant emotional values to you during your day.
  • After you finished your daily activities, you will be interacting with virtual postcards within a smart phone application. These postcards act as a self-reflection after the day is finished. There will be catchphrases and questions provided to provoke reflection by the participants.
  • A follow-up interview session will be held after the data collection period of one week is finished. This session will be audiotaped.

What will happen if I take part?

  • How long will the participant be involved: One week to use the diary system, and one following interview session that lasts 45 minutes.
  • How often will the participants meet the researcher : 2 times. The initial briefing and the follow- up interview
  • What is the research method used : Cultural probes, a novel collection of techniques to gain prominence in interactive systems design, is used as the research method. Introduced by Bill Gaver, the method was designed to provoke inspirational responses during a design process. Participants will be given a set of artefacts, called probes, for them to interact with during the observation period.

Other Consent Bits

What are the possible disadvantages and risks of taking part?

A really small distraction during your daily activities. You will spend time on writing personal self- reflection during the observation period and on answering questions during follow-up interview session.

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

We will provide you with the result and analysis of the study. When we publish an academic paper based on the result, we will also provide you the access to the paper. As an appreciation of your time, you will be offered £15 of Amazon Gift Card. Your recorded responses will be really helpful to design and develop a new interactive technology to support remote empathy communication.

What will happen when the research study stops?

The collected data will be destroyed after 12 months following the completion of my PhD.

Will my taking part in the study be kept confidential?

Any identifying data collected will be anonymised. Participant names will not be associated with the recordings or any other data, and will not appear on any reports or presentations. All data will be password protected, stored securely, and backed up. Only myself and my supervisors will have access to the data. Participants have right to withdraw from the study at any time, and I will destroy all of their collected data when they decide to do so.

What will happen to results of the research study?

The result of this study will be analysed and will be a part of my PhD transfer report and PhD thesis. Insights and ideas extracted from the result will be used to develop a novel system to support remote empathy communication.

What will happen if I don’t want to carry on with the study?

You are free to withdraw from the study without an explanation or penalty at any time.
